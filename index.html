THIS WEEK'S REVIEW: GAME OF THRONES

This week, we decided to weigh in on a television classic that has stirred up admiration, fandoms and controversy over one of the most contested finales to date.

IMDb has given Game of Thrones (GoT) an aggregate rating of 9.2 out of 10 and we thought it would be interesting to analyse the interest amongst male and female viewers across different age groups

MEN'SRATING OF GoT

WOMEN'S RATING OF GoT

The graphs show that generally, men and women across most of the age groups (under 18, 18 to 29 years old, 30 to 44 years old and older than 45 years old) have rated the show between 9 and 10 out of 10. However, women aged under 18 years old rated the series an average of 7.4 out of 10. We think the sexualisation and violence against women may be jarring for teenage girls and this could contribute to the dip in ratings. Despite the dip in rating, the show is generally favoured by most audiences and the team here at the Popcorn Crew definitely recommend this cult classic to our readers. Yes, it is a fantasy, war, period piece but it has themes that can appeal to many people and it brings the suspense and drama from the first episode.

A Critical Reflection on the Design is Your Website, its UI and its UX

My website is centred around film and will display the top film releases each week (or two weeks). I plan on comparing sales data from 2018 (before the pandemic) to those in 2021 (a year into the pandemic) and portray the effect that the pandemic has had on films and the amount of money they earn in the first week of box office sales. The idea is to summarise and comment on the economic trends in film. In an attempt to get a better scope, I have brainstormed analysing a specific genre or franchise of films such as the ones from the Marvel Cinematic Universe in order to analyse a smaller group of ewers and their interest in the films before and during the pandemic. I plan on using the IMDb application programming interface (API) which provides movie and television (TV) information, IMDb ratings, metascore ratings, directs and other data.

User Experience (UX) principles state that websites should have a clear hierarchy and I have done this by prioritising the film data and visuals and having the theory blogs act as secondary information that would not b e placed at the top of the webpage. Another key element is having reactive features on your website so viewers get feedback and see their actions in real time. I plan on doing this through having a hover feature on the navigation bar links and to also have a permanent hover on the menu item/page that has been selected on the website so users can keep track of which webpage they are on.

The user interface (UI) deals with specific assets such as colour, typography and images that the user will interact with. I am still playing around with my colour scheme but, because the website will be name "Popcorn Crew" I thought to make use of yellow for the effect of buttered popcorn and the purple colour would act as a complimentary colour as per the colour wheel but I was also considering using red to represent the red curtains in a cinema. With regards to the typography, I have made use of bold headings and possibly a font with a similar feel to a typewriter for the articles to give the user and "insider feel" to the content and have them feel as though they are behind the scenes with the film community.

I plan on using images and may embed videos of the trailers and reviews of the films being discussed. I would like to also embed videos that discuss the trends in film releases so that viewers can have video data that supports the data visualisation.

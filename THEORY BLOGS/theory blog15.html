REFLECTION ON MY DATA-DRIVEN ARTWORK

The data-driven artwork used on my website was inspired by the idea of creating artwork that could resemble popcorn. I thought that this would tie in nicely with the theme of the website and keep the idea of critiquing films and television shows fun and accessible. As mentioned in my rationale, the colour code of the website is navy blue, to mimic the dark cinema rooms, with accents of yellow and orange to imitate buttered popcorn and to add a contrast and pop of colour to the website.

I struggled with executing the idea behind this artwork but I wanted to make the radius of the circles within the bubble diagram to be directly proportional to the ratings that they have on the Jikan API. This would help viewers with detecting which series is the best rated (Implied relationship) (Diakopoulos, 2018) and could possibly influence the order in which they engage with the series. I wanted the interaction component to be in the form of a description or image that would pop up when the mouse hovered over each data point. The pop-up feature would ideally have a poster of the series, the name and the rating that it has been given.

Despite the data coming from a different dataset, I wanted the artwork to tie in with the theme of engaging with film and television reviews and finding ways to engage and possibly critique them. This website often discusses English language films and television shows and one could argue that it is biased towards them. I wanted to create an artwork that interrogated our biases and “defaults” (Diakopoulos, 2018) and that is why I felt that it was important for the website to recommend films from different film communities and I decided to do this by exploring some of the "top rated" anime shows.

The radiuses of the data points are not accurate but are proportional and I did this to create data points that could catch the eye more easily. A radius that would have been 7.2 was illustrated as 72 to be more visible. Yoshiyiki Mikami and Joshua Smith also adapted the number of pixels to animals on the endeared species list ratio to reflect the number of animals left while still being able to be represents is a 7x7 or 8x8 image (DL Cade. 2019).

HISTORICAL DATA VISUALISATION

The Rose Chart was a data visualisation used by Florence Nightingale during the Crimean War that occurred from October 1853 to February 1856. The purpose of the chart was to portray the different causes of death for each month during the war. The chart depicted deaths from preventable or mitigable zymotic diseases (signified by the blue), deaths from wounds (signified by the red) and deaths from other causes (signified by the black). The black line across the red triangle in November of 1854 signified the boundary of the deaths from all other deaths during the month. The description also states that in October of 1854 and April of 1855, the black area coincided with the red and in January and February of 1856, the blue coincided with the black.

This diagram was designed during a period of time described as "The Golden Age of statistical graphics" (1850-1900). During this period, growth in the importance of "numerical information for social planning, industrialisation, commerce and transportation occurred" (Friendly. 2008). Graphics in the form of geometric shapes that could illustrate volume or movement were developed. When looking at this chart and its description, the use of geometric shapes and colour as opposed to using drawing of people is what stood out to me. The geometric shapes helped with communicating in a clearer way. However, it took me a while to understand what the scale of the data was due to quantities not being present and the presence of a key would have communicated what the sections of the charts were representing a lot clearer than the description that has been displayed in cursive font. This chart reads as 3 different pie charts that have been layered on top of each other and I wonder if a bar graph would have communicated the number of deaths and their causes clearer.

In "Theories of Representation", Kidd suggests that infographics have become increasingly complex and that is why they need to be simple in their representation so that viewers are able to understand and interpret them. Representation can be seen as the description or portrayal of something and, in the case of data visualisation, is seen as the data that is synthesised and depicted. Representation has a "frustrated relationship with reality" (Kidd 2015) due to the fact that it needs to be portrayed in a way that is accurate and understood by many people. I see and feel this frustration when trying to understand the rose chart as the accuracy of the information is unclear and be interpreted in many ways. An example of this would be in the fact that the visibly larger surface is of the blue section of the diagram could be interpreted as an increase in quantity or the number of people. Had the chart had numbers and values present, the number of deaths that Nightingale was trying to track could have been communicated in a clearer way.

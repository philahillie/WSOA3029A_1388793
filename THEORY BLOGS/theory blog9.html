INTERACTIVITY AS A SEMOITIC DEVICE

Semiotics or semiology refers to the study of signs, symbols and signification. A signifier can be seen as any material thing that signifies while something that is signified is the concept that a signifier refers to. For example, the word "rose" is a signifier whilst the actual rose flower is the signified object. The signifier and signified make up the sign which is the smallest unit of meaning. Signs can be symbolic – the relation between signifier and signified is conventional and culturally specific such as most words – or signs can be iconic where the signifier and signified resemble each other (i.e., a picture). (University of Vermont)

Interactivity refers to the process of two things working together and influencing each other and this is similar to the idea of signifiers and signified objects making up signs that can be used to communicate meanings. I understand interactivity to be the process of transforming an input – which are the actions of the user through the use of a mouse or interpretation – into an output that is produced by the computer (Dimara and Perin). Therefore, I see interactivity as a semiotic device through the fact that interactivity produces signs through the collaboration of signifiers and signified objects or actions.

In the example provided by InformationIsBeautiful.net is a dataset that displays which fish are okay to eat. The visualisation allows users to choose a category that they care about at the top of the visuals. Users can choose whether they care about which fish has toxins, are cruel to catch, cause environmental harm or animal welfare. Initially, I thought that clock on these categories would result in the fish that pertain to that classification being highlighted while the other fish would be less colourful or place in the background. The types of fish that apply to each category are signified through the user hovering their mouse over each category or set of words that can be seen as the signifier. In this case, the signified and signifier communicated for information about the fish which have represented in the table through the use of iconic signs (pictures) and symbolic signs (words). The focus has been drawn to the "YES", "MAYBE" and "NO" symbolic signs on the left and the iconic signs on the right which are the different species of fish. Colour and its intensity have been used to highlight the "YES" and "NO" categories while the "MAYBE" category has been shown as grey and muted. This makes the definitive yes and no categories easier to read.

The secondary focus is on the second set of classifications that divide the fish into farmed, Atlantic only, Atlantic and Pacific and Pacific only fish. This information then corresponds with the tertiary element which is the interactive hover device that has been added. This visualisation is interesting and informative but I do wish that the interactive hover part of the visualisation was larger so that it was easier to engage with and read.

ANALYSIS OF A CASE STUDY FROM INFORMATIONISBEAUTIFUL.NET

The diagram I decided to analyse was one that detailed how the human papillomavirus (HPV) vaccine has helped decrease HPV infections in sexually active females over a period of eleven years (2003 to 2014). The diagram states that from 2003 to 2006, the prevalence of HPV infections among sexually active women aged between 14 and 24 was at 54%. However, the prevalence of the HPV infection dropped significantly to 0.9% between 2011 and 2014. This graph does a good job at communicating the story and what is being communicated to viewers. It makes great use of visual hierarchy as the viewer is drawn to the large font of the title that informs the viewer about what is being discussed and further highlights the word "infections" through the use of a different but bright colour. The colour that "infections" has been highlighted in is the same colour that the numerical value of the infections has been displayed in the diagram thus one is able to conclude that the infections and the percentages are linked.

The subheading states that the information applies to a percentage of sexually active females who are aged between 14 and 24 years old. This subheading gives the data context but is displayed in a slightly darker colour so that it is not the first thing that grabs he attention of viewers. This same principle is applied to the circular diagrams as a way of displaying the percentages of infections within the context of 100% so people can see just how much the percentage of infections has decreased. The "efore vaccine" and "after vaccine" is displayed in a font that is not as bold as the main title but is still just as bright as it is an important piece of information that tracks the progress of the vaccine of the eleven years (which have been de-emphasized).

The diagram that illustrates the amount of infects between 2003 and 2006 is smaller than the second diagram as the authors want to draw a focus on how the vaccine has essentially helped in mitigating infections – hence the second diagram being larger. I think this is also to ensure that the smaller percentage is still visible and does not get lost or overshadowed by the bigger percentage in the smaller diagram. While the background is inviting and fun, I wonder if it is necessary. It does not detract from the information and the understand of the information but if it were placed in a website between other text, it might be better if the background was plain so that more attention could be drawn to the data.

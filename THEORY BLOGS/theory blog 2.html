A CLOSE ANALYSIS OF THE UX AND UI DESIGN OF THE SUPERBALIST WEBSITE

Superbalist is an online store and website that I use often. The website sells clothing, accessories, furniture and electronics. It took me a fairly long time to trust and consider online shopping as an option so, when I first discovered the website, I used it as a way of seeing what clothes or shoes I was interested in possibly buying in stores and how much I would need to budget for them. Superbalist acted as a browser for clothes and shoes because it had an efficient searching and filter system that helped me find what I was looking for and also recommended similar options that I might also like.

User Experience (UX) can be understood as the purpose and functionality of the product. Good UX reduces complaints from customers and is supposed to increase a customerâ€™s trust in the brand. When I eventually decided to use the online shopping services, I felt comfortable with trusting them with my money because I had seen my peers purchase from them and receive their order promptly and in good condition. I also appreciated the follow up emails and communication throughout the delivery process. UX can also refer to the structure of the website and the way products are coded and displayed. Superbalist relies on visuals to display the products they are selling. They also have filtering systems that help users have better access to products for men, women and home products such as furniture.

User Interface (UI) can be understood as the design attributes that determine how easy a website or product is to engage with and how well it performs. The Superbalist user interface is very easy to engage with and I enjoy the many options one has when it comes to deciding on the type of product you would like to purchase (electronics, shoes, clothes etc.), the type of brand you would like (through the use of filter functions on the site) and through being able to place items in a cart or on a wish list so you can purchase them later. I also appreciate their wait list feature where they email you when an item that was sold out is now available for purchase.

Kidd discusses representation and how it can be shown through language or, in the case of Superbalist, through the fact that this online store is compiled of brands that are readily available to South Africans and thus will not require interacting with customs shipping or the fear of your items not making it to you promptly. Superbalist also makes an effort to make their sales known to their customers instead of hiding them and this is something I appreciate when looking for items on a tighter budget.

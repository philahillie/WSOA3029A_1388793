OVERVIEW OF DATA VISUALISATION

Data can be defined as unprocessed information and information can be viewed as data that has been processed. Data can be symbols or "computerised representations" of unprocessed information that has been collected and has no significance or meaning until it has been processed into a form of information that can be understood by people. Data can exist as primary or secondary data. Primary data is data that is collected from units or individual respondents and used for a specific study of information such as a census study. Secondary data has information that can be used from records, processed and statistically analysed to get information for multiple purposes. Primary data is considered raw data and secondary data is considered processed data (information).

Information refers to processed data that can be understood by and useful to people. It is collected and represented through numbers, graphical images and natural and artificial languages. It provides answers to questions and is given meaning through its ability to relatively connect data to people.

Data visualisation can be described as the use of graphical images of data or concepts that involves the exchange of information. This information can be used to help people make informed decisions and also process information that is relevant to the content they are engaging in. Data visualisation relies on computing technology and the connection between it and data in order to transform data into information that can be used by users. The use of interactive visual representations of data assist in the understanding of it. in Data visualisation exists in two forms, information visualisation and scientific formation. Information visualisation is the process of representing abstract data in a visual way so that it can be understood by users. Scientific visualisation is the graphical representation of data as a means of obtaining comprehensive insight into scientific data and can also refer to visual data analysis.

Information visualisation can be categorised in three parts and they are information as an organised arrangement of facts (i.e., a map), information presented as a means of understanding a process (i.e., a guide book) and the design of control systems (i.e., feedback controls of a product). Information visualisation is also seen as information design and information design prioritises designing an efficient communication of information.

Scientific visualisation can be expressed through graphical forms such as simulations, waveforms or volumes. These representations help convey data that has been explored and transformed to gain insight. It is common for scientific visualisation to use 3D modelling represent data that has several dimensions.

Readability, recognisability and meaning are important components of good data visualisation. Human perception also plays a big role in data visualisation and can improve the quality and quantity of information that is being displayed. Perception looks at the process of being aware of, gathering and storing and interpreting information. It links human senses to information and helps is the process human undergo to interpret the world around us. It helps us overcome the ambiguous nature of the world and the data within it.

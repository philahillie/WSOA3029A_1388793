CRITICAL REFLECTION ON MY OWN INTERACTIVE DATA

Interactivity looks at how two things can work together and influence each other. It also looks at the ability of a computer to respond to a user's input (Oxford Languages). My first two graphs looked at the statistics of men and women who watched Game of Thrones, a series adapted from George R. R. Martin’s novels and produced through HBO. I decided to divide the data into four ages groups – teenagers, young adults, adults and older adults. I used a scatter plot format to plot the data and represented the two genders with different colours so people could see that the graphs were representing different things

In retrospect, I would have liked for these graphs to be line graphs so that people could see the dips in ratings more accurately. This would minimise the time it takes to interpret the values that have been plotted. When I first read the brief for the graphs, I thought I could create two of the same graphs as long as they represented different data but was mistaken. I should have placed the data in the same graph but possibly had two different polylines that could clearly represent the similarities and differences of interest between men and women in different age groups. Additional labels at the tail end of the polylines would then be used so viewers could see which line represented men and women.

With regards to interactivity, once could add commentary or quotes that could help affirm why the different genders and age groups were the way that they were. For example, female teenagers were recorded as liking Game of Thrones the least and this could be due to the violence and sexual abuse that has been displayed in the show. I could attach short quotes or additional information that would make users understand why female teenagers would not be the target audience for a show like this.

I enjoy the use of colour as it was easy to differentiate from the x and y axes and the data that had been plotted. It was also easy to know which graph I was looking at as the colours were different in each one. However, this use of colour would have been more apparent in a polyline or bar graph.
